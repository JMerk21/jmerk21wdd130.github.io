Go Hiking!

Warmer weather is highly recommended

Look up local hikes in your area and go!

You can go further too, but requires more planning

Destination hikes can be more satisfying to do

Most of the time, they should be free, but prepare a lunch ahead of time

Don't forget hydration! It's VERY important!!

Stargaze

If you have a truck, you can go park out far away from lights

If you don't have a truck, you can bring blankets and pillows and sprawl out under the beautiful night sky.

Hammocking can work too, but your vision of the sky might be obscured

You can make a date out of it! Find the different constellations with your date!

Be a Tourist in your Own City!

There are many ways to explore your city!

  • Look at historical buildings
  • Check out various landmarks in your area
  • Hit up some visitors centers and see what they recommend

Some places could cost money to enter, so if you're okay spending money, verify how much it's going to cost you. If not, avoid those places.

Snowboard/Ski/Sledding

Snowboarding and Skiing are some fun outdoor winter dates!

If you don't have a snowboard or skis already, sledding is easy and relatively inexpensive too!

If you don't already have the equipment, it could get quite expensive so be aware of that

Most ski resorts have rentals available to help offset some of the cost
